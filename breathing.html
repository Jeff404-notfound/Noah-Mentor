Deep Breathing Exercises for Mental Health

Deep breathing exercises are a simple, scientifically-backed way to calm the mind and body. They activate the body's parasympathetic nervous system, helping reduce physical symptoms of stress and anxiety.

  1. Diaphragmatic Breathing (Belly Breathing)
    How to do it: Lie on your back or sit comfortably. Place one hand on your chest and the other on your belly. Inhale slowly and deeply through your nose, feeling your belly rise while your chest stays still. Exhale slowly through your mouth.
    Scientific Backing: Reduces stress and cortisol levels; commonly taught in stress management programs.
  2. Box Breathing (Square Breathing)
    How to do it: Inhale for 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Repeat.
    Scientific Backing: Used by Navy SEALs; helps regulate the nervous system and clear the mind.
  3. Pursed-Lip Breathing
    How to do it: Inhale slowly through your nose, then exhale slowly through pursed lips for a count of six or more.
    Scientific Backing: Keeps airways open, slows breathing, improves oxygen exchange, and calms anxiety.

  5. Alternate Nostril Breathing (Nadi Shodhana)
    How to do it: Inhale and exhale alternately through each nostril while closing the other.
    Scientific Backing: Reduces physiological stress and balances mind and body.
  6. Coherent Breathing (Resonance Breathing)
    How to do it: Breathe at 5 breaths/min (inhale 6, exhale 6).
    Scientific Backing: Synchronizes heart rate variability; promotes balanced nervous system.
  7. Progressive Relaxation with Breath
    How to do it: Inhale and tense a muscle group, hold, exhale and release. Repeat for other muscles.
    Scientific Backing: Combines tension/release with breathing to reduce body tension and anxiety.
  8. Mindful Breathing
    How to do it: Sit quietly, notice your breath, and gently bring focus back if the mind wanders.
    Scientific Backing: Core part of mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR); reduces rumination and improves emotional regulation.
